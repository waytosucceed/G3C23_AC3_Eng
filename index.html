Adjective vs Adverb


Convention/Grammar

Adjectives describe nouns, answering the question "What kind?"
They provide details about people, places, or things, like happy, red, or tall.

Adverbs describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, answering "How?" "When?" "Where?" or "To what extent?"
They provide details about actions, qualities, or other descriptors, like quickly, gently, or always.

Many adverbs are formed by adding "-ly" to the end of an adjective. quick (adjective) → quickly (adverb)

Let us take a look at this table to understand “Adjective vs Adverb”:

Adjective Adverb
Describe nouns (people, places, or things) or pronouns Describe verbs (action words), adjectives, or other adverbs
Answer the question: "What kind?" Answer the question: "How?" "When?" "Where?" "To what extent?"
Examples: happy, red, tall, short, smart Examples: quickly, carefully, gently, patiently
Examples in sentences:
  • The happy dog.
  • The shiny ball.
  • The tall tree.
 Examples in sentences:
  • The dog chased quickly.
  • The student wrote carefully.
  • The tree swayed gently.
